X-LingualTTS: Expressive Cross-Lingual TTS for Indian Languages via Semantic Style Matching and Chain-of-Thought Prompt Injection

Anonymous Submission

Expressive speech synthesis is essential for applications such as dubbing, storytelling, and interactive media, yet remains underexplored in low-resource, multilingual contexts such as Indian languages. In this paper, we propose X-LingualTTS, a cross-lingual expressive text-to-speech (TTS) framework for five diverse Indian languages, namely, Hindi, Indian English, Bengali, Tamil, and Telugu. Our approach introduces three key innovations: (1) a set of style-adaptive and shift layers that condition the speech generation process on both linguistic and acoustic prosody cues; (2) chain-of-thought prompt injection, where a large language model generates non-vocalized expressive tags (e.g., <Laugh>, <Sigh>, <Groan>) to serve as soft emotional controllers; and (3) a semantic consistency loss that aligns prosodic style embeddings derived from text and audio. We evaluate X-LingualTTS in a cross-lingual prosody transfer setting, where expressive speech in Indian languages is synthesized using reference speech in English. Experimental results demonstrate that the system produces perceptually rich and emotionally coherent speech, even under zero-shot language conditions. This work advances the modeling of controllable, cross-modal expressive TTS for linguistically diverse and low-resource environments.
